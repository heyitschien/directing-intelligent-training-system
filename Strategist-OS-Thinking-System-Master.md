## Step 1 · 🧭 DEFINE (Yi)

**Summary:** Set direction before solving. This is your intent, your desired end state, and your reason for entering the problem at all.

**Lead:** Define answers one question first: **What am I trying to accomplish?** Without this step, intelligence becomes scattered. With it, thinking gains direction.

**Callout:** 👉 **Yi is your chosen aim.** It is not yet the system itself. It is the intention you bring into reality: the outcome, the time horizon, the constraints, and the standard for success.

### 🧪 What Define must answer

- 🎯 What is the outcome I want?
- 📌 Why does it matter?
- ⚙️ What are my real constraints?
- 📊 How will I know this worked?

### ✍️ Good copy for your own prompts

“I want to achieve **X** within **Y** conditions, while optimizing for **Z**.”

> “Intent sets direction. If the destination is vague, even perfect maps will not save you.”

↓

## Step 2 · 🧩 MODEL (Li)

**Summary:** See the structure of reality. This is where you stop reacting to surface events and begin seeing the system producing them.

**Lead:** Modeling is the discipline of asking: **What system am I operating in?** This is the heart of structured thinking. Inside Model lives the full five-part framework you practiced.

**Callout (green):** 👉 **Important distinction:** the goal in Define and the goal inside Model are related, but not identical. Define is *your chosen objective*. The goal inside Model is *what this system is actually designed to do*.

### Model framework (GCICL — nested in app)

#### 🎯 Goal

**Q:** What is this system actually trying to achieve?

This is the system-level aim. It must describe the function of the system, not just your personal wish. A weak answer is vague. A strategist answer names quality, duration, and real-world output.

- 👉 Weak: “Have energy”
- 👉 Strong: “Sustain high-quality energy throughout the day”

#### ⚙️ Components

**Q:** What are the parts that make this system what it is?

Components include visible parts like people, tools, processes, and time blocks, and hidden parts like emotional state, structure, trust, incentives, and rhythm. If you miss key components, your strategy will be built on an incomplete map.

#### 🔄 Interactions

**Q:** How do the parts affect one another over time?

Interactions create chain reactions and feedback loops. This is where patterns come from. Strategists do not merely list variables; they trace how one condition produces another.

- Positive loop: clarity → progress → confidence → stronger execution
- Negative loop: confusion → mistakes → frustration → avoidance

#### 🚧 Constraints

**Q:** What limits the performance of the system?

Constraints are the reality you must design within: time, energy, environment, tools, rules, training level, attention, fatigue, and available resources. Strategy is not fantasy. Strategy is design under real conditions.

#### 🔑 Leverage

**Q:** Where does small change create disproportionate impact?

Leverage is where strategy lives. The strategist hunts for the 20% of variables that drive 80% of outcomes. If you see leverage, you stop wasting effort and begin moving systems.

*Chips:* Not brute force · Not random action · Targeted influence

> “Do not fight problems directly. Find the system producing them.”

↓

## Step 3 · ⚔️ INTERROGATE

**Summary:** Question the system until leverage becomes visible. This is the bridge between raw information and extracted intelligence.

**Lead:** After you model the system, you interrogate it. You ask sharper questions than surface thinking would ever ask. This is how elite questioning turns a model into strategy.

### 🔥 Five elite question types

- **🧱 First principles:** What are the fundamental truths here?
- **🔍 Blind spots:** What am I not seeing?
- **⚙️ Lever identification:** What matters most?
- **🚀 Optimization:** How do we make this 10x better?
- **🧭 Strategic path:** What is the most efficient sequence of moves?

### ✍️ Copywriting pattern

“Given this model, what is most likely to break, what is highest leverage, and what move changes the whole field?”

**Callout (amber):** 👉 A weak user asks, “What should I do?” A strategist asks, “What are the leverage points, risks, and highest-probability sequence?”

↓

## Step 4 · ⚡ EXECUTE

**Summary:** Turn leverage into motion. Action matters, but only after direction and structure are clear.

**Lead:** Execution is the sequence of moves that acts on leverage. Good execution is not frantic. It is chosen, ordered, and measurable.

### ⚡ Execution questions

- ➡️ What is the next move?
- 🔢 What order should actions happen in?
- 📈 Which action has the highest upside with lowest friction?
- 📊 How will I measure whether this worked?

**Callout (red):** 👉 Strategy without action is philosophy. Action without strategy is noise. Execution is where insight becomes reality.

↓

## Step 5 · 🧘 REFLECT

**Summary:** Refinement turns one action into accumulated wisdom. Reflection is how the strategist becomes sharper over time.

**Lead:** Reflection asks whether your action moved reality closer to the aim. This is how you stop repeating mistakes and start building judgment.

### 🧘 Reflection prompts

- ✅ What worked?
- ⚠️ What failed?
- 🔄 What changed in the system?
- ✨ What should I keep, remove, or refine?

**Callout (purple):** 👉 Calm reflection is not hesitation. It is the discipline that converts experience into foresight.

# 🧪 Worked examples (three systems — workshop copy)

**How to use:** Each example walks **Goal · Components · Interactions · Constraints · Leverage** (Step 2 in practice).

---

## Example 1 · ⚡ Personal Energy System

**Scenario:** Some days you wake up clear and energized. Other days you feel foggy, distracted, and low-energy. You want to understand why your energy fluctuates and how to stabilize it.

- **🎯 Goal:** Sustain high-quality physical, mental, and emotional energy throughout the day.
- **⚙️ Components:** Sleep quality and timing; nutrition timing and food quality; hydration; movement and exercise; stress and emotional state; environment: light, noise, routine, space.
- **🔄 Interactions:** Poor sleep → lower energy → weaker food choices → bigger crash later; good morning routine → stronger momentum → calmer mood → better output; stress → mental fatigue → lower clarity → less effective decisions.
- **🚧 Constraints:** Work schedule; physical fatigue; limited time; environmental inconsistency.
- **🔑 Leverage:** Sleep timing consistency; first meal quality; early hydration; simple morning routine.

> 👉 The lesson: do not label yourself “low energy.” Find the system conditions producing the feeling.

---

## Example 2 · 📐 Learning System (ASVAB / Math)

**Scenario:** Some topics click quickly, some feel difficult, retention is inconsistent, and your performance varies. You want to understand how your learning process works so you can improve it.

- **🎯 Goal:** Build deep understanding and reliable problem-solving speed under test conditions.
- **⚙️ Components:** Concept understanding; input: lessons and explanations; practice problems; feedback and correction; progression structure; focus level and emotional state.
- **🔄 Interactions:** Clear explanation → better practice → confidence → stronger retention; confusion → mistakes → frustration → avoidance → slower growth; good feedback → corrected thinking → higher accuracy next round.
- **🚧 Constraints:** Limited time; mental fatigue; topics with uneven difficulty; performance pressure.
- **🔑 Leverage:** Principle → Example → Practice; progressive drills from easy to elite; immediate feedback; extracting the pattern after each problem.

> 👉 The lesson: learning improves fastest when the system is designed well, not when effort is merely increased.

---

## Example 3 · 🤖 You + AI System

**Scenario:** Sometimes AI gives powerful insights, sometimes answers feel generic, and sometimes you do not know what to ask. You want to consistently extract high-quality intelligence.

- **🎯 Goal:** Consistently extract high-quality, actionable intelligence that improves thinking, decisions, and execution.
- **⚙️ Components:** You: clarity, structure, patience, context; AI: reasoning, pattern synthesis, response generation; prompt quality; response quality; feedback loop and refinement.
- **🔄 Interactions:** Clear prompt → strong answer → better thinking → stronger next prompt; vague prompt → generic answer → confusion → weaker follow-up; more context → more targeted reasoning → more useful output.
- **🚧 Constraints:** Unclear thinking; lack of structure; limited context; fatigue and attention limits.
- **🔑 Leverage:** Clear intent before asking; structured prompts; iterating instead of accepting the first answer; asking for principles, blind spots, and leverage points.

> 👉 The lesson: AI often mirrors the quality of your thinking. Better input creates better intelligence.

# 🧠 Foundational principles (Strategist OS — condensed from master doc)

## I. 🧭 First principle: clarity of intent (Yi → 意)

> “What is the outcome you seek… precisely?”

### 🔥 This is where most people fail

- Weak: “Help me build a business” / “Give me ideas”
- Strong: “Design a system that generates $10k/month within 6 months using skills X, constraints Y, time Z.”

👉 **Specific intent compresses infinite possibilities into actionable strategy.**

### 🧪 Training (every time)

- 🎯 Outcome · ⏱ Time horizon · ⚙️ Constraints · 📊 Success metric

---

## II. 🧩 Second principle: structured thinking (Li → 理)

### 🧠 The “3-layer thinking model”

1. **SYSTEM** — components & interactions  
2. **LEVERS** — variables that move the outcome  
3. **STRATEGY** — sequence of actions that change those variables  

### 🔥 Example

- ❌ Normal: “Grow my YouTube channel”
- ✅ Strategist: System (content → distribution → retention → monetization) · Levers (CTR, watch time, frequency) · Strategy (test thumbnails → retention → scale winners)

---

## III. ⚔️ Third principle: elite questioning

Patterns: first principles · what’s missing · where it breaks at scale · 10x better.

*(Aligned with the five types in Step 3 above.)*

---

## IV. 🧘 The Zhuge Liang layer (mindset)

- Calm under pressure · long-term thinking · precision over noise · discipline in execution  
- **Shift:** Don’t rush to answers — sit with the problem longer than others.  
- AI gives speed; this layer gives **depth + timing**.

---

## V. 🧠 The convergence — your thinking flow

1. **DEFINE (Yi)** — What exactly am I trying to achieve?

3. **INTERROGATE** — Leverage? Missing pieces?

# 🏬 Extended case study — retail front end (“Whole Foods” style)

**Core question:** *“What system is producing these outcomes?”*

**5-part lens:** Goal · Components · Interactions · Constraints · Leverage *(same template as above).*

### 1. True goal (not shallow)

- Weak: “Sell groceries.”  
- Strong: *Move customers through the experience smoothly, accurately, and pleasantly while maintaining employee energy and operational efficiency.*

### 2. Main components (visible + invisible)

Employees, customers, managers, registers, schedules, breaks, policies, communication, layout, tech/POS, rush demand, morale, training, etc.

### 3. Interactions (chain reactions)

Energy ↔ service quality ↔ customer mood ↔ stress ↔ workflow ↔ staffing ↔ leadership tone — strategists look for **chain reactions**, not isolated blame.

### 4. Constraints

Staffing limits, schedules, rush spikes, fatigue, space, policy, skill variance, tech bottlenecks, manager bandwidth.

### 5. Leverage (examples)

Break timing · staffing vs. rush windows · line monitoring · role clarity · calm leadership under pressure · communication quality · small workflow fixes.

### Practice upgrade (analysis → action)

Ask: **easiest to influence?** · **biggest upside?** · **most realistic under constraints?**

> Do not fight the visible problem first. Find the system producing the problem.
